title: "Platform Fees"
---

Rain uses a fee structure to sustain the platform and incentivize participants, including market creators, liquidity providers, and resolvers.

**Standard Fees**

A fee of 5% is collected from each market's trading volume. These fees are automatically distributed to various participants within the ecosystem:

- **Market Creator**: Receives 1.2%
- **Liquidity Providers**: Receive 1.2%
- **Resolver**: Receives 0.1%
- **\$RAIN Token Buyback-and-Burn**: 2.5% of the market's trading volume is used to buy back and burn \$RAIN tokens. This acts as a deflationary mechanism, linking token scarcity to platform activity and adoption.

Additionally, in public markets, an extra \$1 fee is collected to ensure a minimum payment for the oracle.

**Dispute Fees**

If a participant believes a decided market outcome is incorrect, they can open a dispute. To do so, the disputer must put up a collateral of 0.1% of the market volume or \$1,000, whichever is less. If the disputer wins the dispute, they get their collateral back. If they lose, the collateral goes to the winning party, either the AI oracle Delphi or the market creator, depending on the type of market.

**Gas Fees**

Gas fees (paid in USDT) are required for all transactions on the Arbitrum network, including creating a market, trading options, and filing a dispute. These fees are set by the network and can fluctuate, but gas fees on Arbitrum are generally very minimal.

The gas fees are added to the transactions and deducted from your in-app balance, so you don't need to hold any USDT on Arbitrum in your connected wallet.
