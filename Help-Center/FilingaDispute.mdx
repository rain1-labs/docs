---
title: "Filing a Dispute"
---

If you believe a market's outcome has been decided incorrectly, you can file a dispute to have the decision reviewed. The dispute must be opened within the 1-hour dispute window that opens after the winner is announced. To file a dispute, you must put up collateral, which is calculated as 0.1% of the market's total volume or \$1,000, whichever is less.

**How to File an Initial Dispute**

1. On the market page, after the winner has been decided, locate the **Results** panel on the right.
2. Click the **CREATE DISPUTE** button.
3. A "Create a Dispute" window will open.
4. Enter a **Title** for your dispute.
5. Provide a detailed **Description** explaining your reasoning and why the initial resolution is incorrect.
6. Click **Select Files** to **Upload Evidence** that supports your claim. This can include screenshots, links, or other proof.
7. Once your information is complete, click the **CREATE DISPUTE** button at the bottom of the form.
8. A confirmation pop-up will appear, showing the calculated collateral fee required to open the dispute.
9. Click **YES, CREATE DISPUTE** to pay the collateral and officially submit your case.

\
**What Happens Next: AI Judge (Lex) Review**

Once your dispute is successfully submitted, the case is first handled by **Lex**, an autonomous AI judge developed by Olympus AI. The market status will update to show that it is currently "Under Dispute".

Lex is designed to analyze the claims and evidence submitted by all parties and deliver an initial, impartial ruling.

**How to Appeal (Escalation to Human Oracles)**

After Lex delivers its initial ruling, you will have the option to appeal the decision if you still believe it is incorrect.

Filing an appeal escalates the issue from the AI judge to a group of **decentralized human oracles**. This group will conduct a final review and make a binding decision on the market's outcome.

**Collateral Distribution**

The dispute process is designed to reward the correct party.

- **If the final ruling sides with you (the disputer):** You will receive your collateral back.

**If the final ruling upholds the original resolution:** Your collateral is awarded to the original resolver (either the market creator or the AI oracle).
