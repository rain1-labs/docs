title: "Drafting a good market question"
---

### **The Golden Rule: Clarity Creates Confidence**

When you create a market on Rain - whether it's a public market resolved by AI or a private market you resolve yourself - the quality of your question determines the quality of the outcome.

A vague question creates room for interpretation, which leads to unhappy users and disputes. A sharp, fact-based question leads to an instant, undisputed resolution.

Here is the difference between a Vague question and a Fact-Based question.

#### **❌ The Vague Question (Avoid this)**

**"Will Bitcoin hit an all-time high soon?"**

**Why this fails:**

- **"Bitcoin":** On which exchange? Spot or Perp?
- **"All-time high":** According to whose data? CoinMarketCap? Binance?
- **"Soon":** This is subjective. Is "soon" next week or next year?
- **The Result:** Whether it is the AI oracle or you deciding the winner, this question is a trap. Losers will argue that "soon" meant something else, or that the price data was wrong, likely forcing a dispute.

#### **✅ The Fact-Based Question (Do this)**

**"Will the price of Bitcoin (BTC) exceed \$120,000 USDT on Binance by December 31, 2025, at 23:59 UTC?"**

**Why this works:**

- **Clear Asset:** Bitcoin (BTC) - standard ticker.
- **Definitive Metric:** Exceeding \$120,000 USDT (binary yes/no).
- **Verifiable Source:** Binance (a high-volume, public data source).
- **Precise Deadline:** December 31, 2025, at 23:59 UTC.

### **Why this matters for Rain Users**

On Rain, you don't need permission to create a market. That freedom comes with the responsibility of clarity. Clarity is your best defense against disputes, whether the market is resolved by the AI oracle or by you.

**If you use the AI Oracle:** It scans the web using multiple agents to verify the result. If you give it rigid targets (like the example above), it can instantly verify the data against the source without error.

**If you Resolve it Yourself:** Participants need to trust you. If your question is vague, your decision might look biased, prompting users to challenge you by filing a dispute. A clear question protects _you_ by making the outcome obvious to everyone, leaving no room for argument.

**The takeaway:** Think of your question like a GPS destination. If you just type "Italy," you could end up anywhere. If you type a specific street address, you arrive at exactly the right spot, every time.
